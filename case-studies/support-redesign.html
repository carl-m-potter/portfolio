Support Site Redesign

UX & Platform Optimisation at Scale

Improved usability and streamlined publishing workflows across 1,500+ product pages and 43 EMEA markets.

Single update → 1,500+ pages updated across 43 markets

Impact

  • +1% sustained CSAT improvement
  • Reduced time to publish content across 1,500+ pages
  • Eliminated duplicate publishing workflows
  • Improved scalability across 43 markets

Context

The support site enabled users to download manuals, drivers, and firmware, and access help content such as FAQs and tutorials.

It served Canon product owners across EMEA markets.

Problem

  • Users missed key navigation elements (tabs not clearly visible)
  • Pages were unnecessarily long due to spacing and layout issues
  • Backend publishing processes were inefficient and duplicated
  • Content updates required repeated manual effort across multiple pages

While overall navigation structure was validated by an external agency, there were clear usability and scalability issues.

My Role

I identified usability gaps and led UX improvements while collaborating with developers and stakeholders to redesign backend processes and improve scalability across the platform.

Before vs After

Before: Tabs lacked visibility and pages required excessive scrolling.

After: Clear tab hierarchy, improved visibility, and more efficient layout.

Approach

1. Aligning with broader redesign

A full redesign was required to align with updated Canon templates and resolve security risks from legacy components.

2. Improving usability

I redesigned tab navigation to improve visibility, introducing clearer visual hierarchy, icons, and strong active states.

3. Reducing cognitive load

I removed unnecessary spacing and layout inefficiencies, reducing page length and improving content visibility.

4. Streamlining backend systems

I worked with development teams to introduce reusable product lists and standardised content structures.

5. Enabling scalable publishing

Content updates could now be applied once and reflected across 1,500+ pages, eliminating repetitive manual publishing.

System Improvement

Centralised components replaced duplicated workflows, enabling scalable content management across all markets.

Outcome

The redesign delivered an immediate and sustained increase in customer satisfaction, alongside significant improvements in internal efficiency.

Publishing workflows were simplified, reducing time and effort required to maintain content across multiple markets and product lines.
