Translation Cost Reduction

Content Strategy & Operational Optimisation

Reduced localisation costs from £950k to £125k by eliminating duplication and standardising content at scale.

£800k+ annual cost savings through content and process optimisation

Impact

  • £950k → £125k annual localisation cost
  • £800k+ annual savings
  • Achieved within 12–18 months, sustained long-term

Context

Support site content (manuals, drivers, firmware announcements) was translated across multiple EMEA markets.

Translation costs were high due to inconsistent content creation and inefficient processes.

Problem

  • Duplicate content sent for translation multiple times
  • No standardised naming or descriptions
  • Poor use of translation memory
  • Large volumes of unnecessary text in firmware announcements

This resulted in repeated translation costs for identical or similar content.

My Role

I identified inefficiencies in the localisation process and led the redesign of content standards and workflows, collaborating with localisation teams, stakeholders, and global headquarters.

Approach

1. Understanding the system

I worked with the localisation project manager to understand how translation memory functioned and where inefficiencies existed.

2. Standardising content

I created consistent naming and description structures for manuals, drivers, and support content, ensuring repeatable patterns.

3. Eliminating duplication

I identified and removed duplicated content being translated multiple times across workflows.

4. Optimising firmware announcements

I reduced unnecessary text while preserving critical technical information, significantly lowering translation volume.

5. Scaling globally

I collaborated with Canon HQ in Japan to align content delivery with the new standardised approach.

6. Driving adoption

I overcame internal resistance by running a trial on three announcements, demonstrating clear cost savings and securing buy-in.

Before vs After Process

Standardisation and removal of duplication enabled effective use of translation memory, eliminating repeated costs.

Outcome

By the end of 2017, the localisation budget was over £300k under target. By 2018, costs dropped below £250k, with continued optimisation reducing annual spend to £125k.

This resulted in sustained annual savings of over £800k.

Maintaining Quality

Content quality was preserved by ensuring all technical information remained intact. Changes focused on removing duplication and standardising structure, not reducing essential content.
