Support Journey Redesign

Reduced support demand and improved self-service

Simplified a complex contact flow and introduced self-service at the point of need, reducing reliance on the contact centre.

Context

The contact support page was designed to route users to the contact centre, but the journey to get help was complex and inefficient.

Users were required to navigate multiple layers of dropdowns to identify their product and issue before being able to proceed.

Problem

  • Up to 5 product selection steps before reaching support
  • Additional steps to define the issue
  • No integration of relevant self-help content

This resulted in a frustrating experience and unnecessary contact centre demand.

My Role

I led the redesign of the support journey, working within CRM constraints to simplify the experience while improving data quality and reducing operational demand.

Approach

1. Simplifying product identification

I replaced complex dropdown structures with a product selector, leveraging an existing component to reduce implementation cost and improve usability.

2. Streamlining issue selection

While constrained by CRM requirements, I removed duplicate and unnecessary options to simplify the journey and improve data accuracy.

3. Introducing self-service at the point of need

I integrated relevant FAQ content directly into the journey, allowing users to resolve common issues without needing to contact support.

4. Enabling scalable content delivery

I created a reusable framework and process for displaying FAQs, reducing the time required to add new content and enabling continuous improvement.

5. Expanding the support ecosystem

The redesign created a foundation for additional improvements, including streamlined repair journeys and further call deflection features.

Following launch, I monitored call volumes and user behaviour to assess impact and identify further optimisation opportunities.

Outcome

  • Immediate reduction in support calls following launch
  • €70K cost reduction in 2021, increasing to €123K by 2022
  • Sustained decrease in contact centre demand over multiple years

While additional factors (such as COVID and the later introduction of live chat) influenced long-term trends, the redesign established a more efficient, self-service-led support journey that contributed to reduced reliance on the contact centre.
