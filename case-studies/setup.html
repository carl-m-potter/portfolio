Setup Optimisation

↓ 25% Wi-Fi-related support calls

Improved discoverability of setup content across 40+ EMEA markets, reducing avoidable support demand.

Context

Canon PIXMA support pages served customers across 40+ EMEA markets, but setup guidance, particularly for Wi-Fi connection, was fragmented across multiple locations (video pages, guides, FAQs).

Despite having relevant content available, customers struggled to find it, leading to avoidable contact centre demand.

Problem

Customers frequently contacted support for basic setup and network connection issues.

  • High call volumes related to setup and Wi-Fi issues
  • Low engagement with existing support content
  • A clear discoverability gap rather than a content gap

My Role

I led the identification of the problem and design of the solution, collaborating with product, engineering, and operations teams to deliver and scale the improvement.

Approach

1. Identifying the root cause

I analysed survey feedback, contact drivers, and Google Analytics data, identifying a mismatch between customer demand for setup help and engagement with existing resources.

2. Reframing the problem

Instead of creating new content, I focused on improving visibility and access to high-value support content.

3. Designing the solution

  • Introduced a dedicated “Setup” tab on product support pages
  • Set as the default landing tab
  • Consolidated key setup journeys (unboxing, Wi-Fi connection)
  • Used accordion components for clarity and scannability

Content was tailored at product-series level to maintain relevance and avoid generic guidance.

4. Balancing usability and scalability

  • Improved customer usability
  • Maintained efficiency for internal teams via CMS-friendly structures

This enabled rollout across multiple markets and product lines.

5. Validating the approach

An A/B test was conducted over a 3-month period to measure impact and confirm effectiveness before wider rollout.

Outcome

  • 25% reduction in Wi-Fi-related support calls for key product lines
  • Improvement sustained over a 12-month period post-launch
  • Increased engagement with setup content

Additionally, the solution introduced a reusable pattern: default tab prioritisation could be applied across other product categories.
